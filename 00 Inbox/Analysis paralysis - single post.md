The most expensive thing in your business isn’t bad marketing.

It’s the website you haven’t launched yet, the offer you’re still “refining,” and the post you wrote and never published because it didn’t feel ready.

Preparation has a point of diminishing returns. At some point, the next Google search, the next course, the next tweak to your homepage isn’t making you more ready. It’s just making you procrastinate more.

The businesses that grow aren’t the ones with the perfect strategy. They’re the ones that shipped something, saw what worked, and tweaked things as they went.

So whatever you’ve been sitting on, this is the sign to put it out.
