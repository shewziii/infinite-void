# Pre-Call Information
**Organization**
Type of organization 
- Size, number of locations
- Products and services offered
- Rank in industry
- Types of customers they serve
    - consumers, retailers, wholesalers
    - Benefits they seek from that organization's products/services
- Competitors
- Publicity
    - What do they believe in and participate in?
- Policies and procedures
    - Meetings, contracts, purchasing, etc.
- Buying process
- People involved in purchase decision
    - Who is Final decision maker?
    - Influencers, gatekeepers, and others involved 

**Individual Buyer**
- Name
- Education 
- Role within organization & career path 
- Social style & personality traits
- Hobbies & interests
- Use LinkedIn and connect

# Pre-Call Objectives (set goals)
**Multiple Objectives**
Help take away the salesperson's fear of failure
- Make sure they align with long-term sales goals
- Set objectives that require a buyer 

---
# Call Objectives
**Optimistic Call Objective**
Most ideal situations and what you hope to accomplish over the long term

**Primary Call Objectives**
Most promising and realistic goal that you wish to achieve

**Secondary Call Objectives**
Goals that would be nice to accomplish but are not top priority, at this time

**Minimum Call Objectives**
Realize that call may not go exactly as planned

# Effective Verbal Messages
**Voice Characteristics**
- Rate of speech
- Loudness
- Inflection
- Articulation

**Stories**
- Paint a word picture
- Include conflicts, trials, & crises

**Effective Use of Words**
Use concrete rather than abstract words
- We ship orders quickly vs We ship orders withing 24 hours after receiving them

**Use Similes**
- Soft as silk
- Built like a tank

**Use Metaphors**
- This machine is a real workhorse

---
# Asking Questions
**Space Them Out**
When salespeople ask several questions, one  right after another, customers  may feel threatened
- Dig into the question and spend time learning what you need to know, "tell me more"

They may feel they are being interrogated
Ask permission to ask questions

**Ask Short Simple Questions**
Avoid asking questions that ask for two different kinds of information

---
# Types of Questions 
**Open Questions**
Questions that keep the conversation going and lets you get to know more about them

**Closed Questions**
Questions that don't really go anywhere. These are good for confirmation. 

---
# 80-20 Listening rule
**Why?**
- Perception that you care
- stimulates and involves speaker in conversation
- mutual problem solving 
- gives you a chance to think
- learn about customer
- lets someone get a problem off their chest 

**Speaking-listening differential**
People can only speak 120-160 words per minute, but can listen to more than 800 words per minute
