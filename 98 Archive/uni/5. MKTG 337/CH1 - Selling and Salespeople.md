# What is Selling?
Selling is about creating value, which is the total benefit that the seller's products and services provided by the buyer.

Economic Exchange
- creates "profit" for both parties
- seller's profit
    - selling price = cost of goods sold and selling costs
    - Buyer's profit = benefit received - (selling price + time and effort to purchase)

---
# What Does a Salesperson Do?
- Creates new customers
- Sells more to present customers
- Build long-term relationships
- Provides solutions to customers' problems
- Provide service to customers
- Helps customers use products after purchase
- Builds goodwill with customers
- Provides company with market information

# How Do You Use Selling Skills in Acquiring a Job?
1. Identify potential employers
2. Analyze employer's needs
3. Demonstrate your ability to satisfy their needs
4. Ask and answer questions during interviews
5. Possibly negotiate starting salary
6. Request commitment (a job) from the employer

---
# Types of Sales Jobs
Retail Selling 
- A retail sales person sells goods or services to consumers for their personal, non-business use

Direct Selling
- Face to face sales to consumers, typically in their homes, who use the products for their non-business personal use

Selling for a wholesaler
- For resale 
- For use in producing other goods
- For use within an organization 

Selling for a Manufacturer
- Working for the firm who manufactures the product 
- Usually, one of the most prestigious jobs to hold 

---
# Advantages of a Sales Position
- Challenge
- Responsibility
- Flexibility in work activities
- Rewards from making a tough sale
- Opportunity for promotion
- Variety and Independence
- Learning the business from the bottom up
- The ability to help people or make a difference
- Compensation potential 
    - Often, the sky is the limit

---
# Disadvantages of a Sales Position
- Limited supervision (need self-motivation)
- Potential conflict between customer and company demands 
- Difficulty handling rejection/lost sales
- Discomfort from asking customers to buy
- Negative stereotypes
- Travel
- Variable income

- Controlling one's emotions

# Important Steps - Relationship Selling Process 
1. Prospecting
	Locating and qualifying prospects 
2. Pre-call planning
	Obtaining interview. Planning: determining sales call objective, developing customer profile, customer benefit program, and sales presentation strategies 
3. Set Meeting
	Phone call or email to schedule a time and place to meet prospect 
4. Approach
	Meeting prospect and beginning customized sales presentation.
5. Needs Identification
	Uncovering needs by asking questions to prospect 
6. Presentation
	Relating product benefits to needs using demonstration, dramatization, visuals, and proof statements.
7. Overcome objections
	Satisfactorily handle any concern prospect has about buying from you
8. Trial Closes
	Asking prospect's opinion after presentation and after overcoming each objection and immediately before the close 
9. Close
	Bringing prospect to the logical conclusion to buy. Ask for commitment 
10. Follow-up and service
	Serving customer after the sale
