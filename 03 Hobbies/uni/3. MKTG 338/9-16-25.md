# What is Web Analytics?
The measurement, collection, analysis and reporting of web data for the purposes of understanding online behaviors.

Consists of:
On site
- behaviors of consumers while on your site

Off site
- behaviors off your site (e.g. email, search engines, affiliate web sites, banner ads, social networks, etc.)

# Key Metrics (KPIs) in Web Analytics
Conversion Rate (CR)
The percentage of users who take the goal action 

# Types of Conversions 
- Email marketing subscriptions
- Contract form completions (= lead generation)
- Clicks to social media marketing 
- Time on site or pages per visit
- Download product guides or watch product description videos
- Purchase a product 

# Website CR to Sales
The majority of e-commerce websites convert on average around 2%, compared to the average physical retail store conversion rate of 30%

# Averate Order Value
The average dollar amount spent each time a customer places an order on a website 

Can be tracked for any time period, buy many companies monitor the monthly average. 

Increase AVO by:
- cross-selling vs upselling strategies
- Discounts or promotions
- Offer free shipping & return for a minimum order value

# Bounce Rate
The percentage of visitors to a particular website who navigat away from the site after viewing only one page 

It is the total number of visits viewing one page only / total number of entrances across all the pages on the website

---
# Landing Page
Appears when a visitor clicks on a search engine result link or on an online advertisement

The general goal of a landing page is to convert site visitors into leads or sales 

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Your home page is not a landing page 

2 types of landing pages
1. Lead Generation landing pages
2. Click through landing page 

Message match
Important part because many visitors will leave your page within a few seconds of arrival if they don't see the message they are looking for in your website 

Example: Online advertisement message "Get 30% off a Samsung Chromebook"

# Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)
Total marketing spend on a particular channel / Number of conversions

---
# Cost per Click (CPC)
Marketing spend on PPC channel($) / Ads clicked(#)

---
# Cost Per Lead (CPL)
pricing model in which the advertiser pays for an explicit sign up from a consumer interested in the advertiser's offer

Total Marketing Spend on a particular campaign and channel / number of completed leads

---
# Cost per Thousand (CPT)
The price of 1000 advertisement impressions on one webpage

Marketing spend on views / number of 1000 impressions
