# Diversification
[[Diversification]] is the process of a company enlarging or varying its produces of field of operation. This decision always merits strong consideration whenever a single business is company encounters diminishing market opportunities and stagnates in sales. Diversifying presents a wide-range of possibilities however, the decision to diversify must start with a  strong economic justification for doing so.

Creating a Diversification strategy is 3 steps
1. pick new industries to enter and decide on the means of entry
2. pursue opportunities to leverage cross-business value chain relationships and strategic fit into competitive advantage
3. Establishing

Diversification is a good idea when the firm can expand into businesses whose technologies and products enhance its present work, when its resources and capabilities can be used as important competitive assets in other endeavors, when overall product costs can be reduced by cross business sharing or transfer of resources and capabilities, and when bringing a valued brand to the produces of other businesses can increase sales and profits

## Common Options to Diversify
- Sticking closely with the existing business lineup and pursuing opportunities presented by these businesses
- Broadening the current scope of diversification by entering additional industries
- Retrenching to a narrower scope of diversification by divesting poorly performing businesses
- Broadly restructuring the entire firm by divesting some businesses and acquiring others to put a whole new face on the firm's business lineup
## Justification for Diversifying
Testing whether diversification will add long term value for shareholders
- Industry attractiveness test
	- Whatever industry you want to move into needs to have better growth potential than your current industry
- Cost of entry test
- The better-off test
## Approaches to Diversification
- Existing business acquisition
- Internal new venture
- Join Venture
## When to Engage in Internal Development
- Ample time to develop and launch business
- Cost of acquisition higher than internal entry
- Added capacity affects supply and demand balance
- Low resistance of incumbent firms to market entry
- Availability of in-house skills and resources
## When to Engage in a Joint Venture
- Is the opportunity too complex, uneconomical, or risky for one firm to pursue alone?
- Does the opportunity require a broader range of competencies and know-how than the firm now possesses?
- Will the opportunity involve operations in a country that requires foreign firms to have a local minority or majority ownership partner?
## Unrelated Diversification
[[Unrelated diversification]] is a business strategy where a company expands into new industries or markets that are not related to its existing core competencies or products. This approach can be riskier but may offer potential for higher returns if successful.
## Related Diversification
[[Related diversification]] is a strategy where a company expands into new industries that are closely related to its existing business. This approach allows firms to leverage their existing resources and expertise, potentially leading to increased efficiency and market advantage.
