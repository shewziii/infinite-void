# Presentations: Value Selling
1. Know your buyer: do research on them and what they are most likely to care about
2. Identify the buyers #1 problem: come ready to show them ways you can provide unique value to them
3. Present your solutions to them in a way that should them you are a partner, not a salesperson

# Trial Closes
Trial closes area a strategic technique used during presentations to gauge a buyer's readiness to make a purchase decision. This is NOT a traditional closing method. These are used throughout a presentation by asking open-ended questions to the buyer that prompt them to share opinions, and reveal their level of interest as a result.

Successful scales professionals check in often
- after educating on a feature, benefit, advantage
- after tying needs/goals/challenges to solutions you can provide
- after explaining information or introducing new materials

# Effective Visuals
- Tell a story
- Have credibility
- Provide proof
- Show them how your product and your company can meet their goals/solve their problems, save them time or money, help mitigate risk or loss
- show how your product and your company relate to their target markets
- have only the information you want your customer to remember on the visuals
