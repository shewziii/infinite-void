**Act One Creative — Social Media Strategy & Brand Consulting**
**Damian (oddthegreat) | March 12, 2026 | 2:00 PM CST**

---

## Before the Call

Review their IG page. Note: bio clarity, content consistency, posting frequency, and whether their credentials are visible. You're walking in with receipts, not assumptions.

Estimated call length: 20–30 minutes. Goal: qualify, connect, and close to next step.

### Damian-Specific Prep

- He already updated his bio after your conversation yesterday. New version is cleaner: lists Logos, Branding, Websites and has a real CTA (DM "PROJECT"). **Acknowledge this on the call.** It shows you're paying attention and sets a collaborative tone.
- Profile photo is still the AI avatar — not the strong professional portrait from his Feb 16 post (dark background, suit, serious look). That post got **30 likes vs 8** on the avatar. The data is already telling him what works. He's just not acting on it.
- The grid is still inconsistent: "Stop Getting Scammed" graphic and Back to Accra event flyers don't match a creative director positioning. They read like side hustles on a portfolio page.
- **Key insight:** he's coachable. He acted on your feedback before the call, without being paid. Lead with that.

---

## Opener (0:00 – 2:00)

**You:** "Hey Damian, appreciate you making time. Before I get into anything, I have to say — I noticed you updated your bio. Already sharper. That tells me you're serious about this."

> Let him respond. This immediately establishes that you're observant, your feedback has value, and he's already benefiting from the relationship before paying a dollar.

**You:** "So let's dig in. You mentioned getting clients — give me a picture of what that looks like for you right now."

> Let him talk. Listen for: what he's currently doing to get work, what's not converting, and how long this has been the problem.

---

## Discovery (2:00 – 12:00)

### The Situation

**You:** "Are you posting consistently, or has that been hard to keep up with?"

**You:** "When someone lands on your page — what do you want them to do?"

> You're checking if they even have a clear CTA in mind. Most don't.

### The Gap

**You:** "So when you look at your page right now, does it communicate that clearly to a stranger?"

> This is the pivot. You're getting him to self-diagnose. Let silence work here.

**You:** "What do you think is getting in the way of people reaching out?"

### The Stakes

**You:** "How long has this been the situation?"

**You:** "What does landing one or two new clients a month actually mean for you — business-wise?"

> You want him to put a number or a feeling on it. That number becomes the value anchor for your offer.

---

## Audit Reveal (12:00 – 18:00)

**You:** "Okay so I've been watching your page closely. And the bio update you made is genuinely better — you've got a service list now, and the CTA is clear. That was the right move."

**You:** "Here's what's left. Your profile photo is still the AI avatar. But that portrait you posted on February 16 — dark background, suit, serious look? That post got 30 likes. The avatar got 8. Your audience already told you which version of you they want to hire. You just haven't made it your face yet."

**You:** "And the grid still has some noise. The 'Stop Getting Scammed' graphic and the Back to Accra flyers — those aren't bad posts, but they don't belong on the same page as someone positioning himself as a creative director. A potential client lands there and can't tell if this is a business page or a personal one."

**You:** "The good news: your credentials are real. Mavin, Tiwa Savage, Cokards. And you're clearly someone who moves fast when given direction. That's actually rare. The page just needs to reflect the person you already are."

> Pause here. Let him react. The goal is for him to feel understood, not critiqued.

---

## The Pitch (18:00 – 24:00)

**You:** "You've already started the work yourself — which is honestly a good sign. What I do is take that further and make it systematic. The right profile photo, a grid strategy that reinforces your creative direction positioning, and a content system so every post is working toward the same goal: getting the right people into your DMs."

**You:** "For you specifically, that looks like: locking in that portrait as your profile photo, building a content framework around your logos, branding, and web work, and clearing the noise so the page tells one clear story."

### Objection: "What does that cost?"

**You:** "My packages start at [your price]. Before I quote you anything specific, I want to make sure I understand your full situation — but what I can tell you is most of my clients start seeing inbound leads within the first 30 days of a repositioned page."

### Objection: "I need to think about it"

**You:** "Totally fair. What's the one thing you'd want to be clearer on before making a decision?"

> Get the real objection. "I need to think about it" is almost never the actual objection.

---

## Close (24:00 – 30:00)

**You:** "Here's what I'd suggest as a next step — I'll put together a quick breakdown of what I'd do for your page specifically, and we can reconnect Thursday or Friday. Does that work?"

> Don't ask "does that sound good?" — ask for a specific day. Vague closes don't close.

**If they're ready now:** "Perfect. I'll send over a short intake form and the agreement today. Once that's done we can get started."

---

## After the Call

- Send a follow-up within 1 hour. Include: what you discussed, what you'd fix, and the next step with a clear deadline.
- If he goes quiet after 48 hours: one short follow-up. Don't chase beyond that.
