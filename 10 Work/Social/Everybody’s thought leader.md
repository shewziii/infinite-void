Everybody’s a thought leader. Which means nobody is.

I know you’ve heard this time and again, but I’ll say it again: we live in an age of information.

Since the release of tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini, people have been able to push content out like crazy. 

That sounds like a win until you look at your feed, which is probably flooded with content that’s technically correct and structured neatly, but is completely forgettable. 

The bar has been set so high that writing something polished is the bare minimum. And audiences can tell when you used AI, too. Or at the very least, they can definitely tell when content reads as generic, flat, or boring.

Don’t get me wrong. AI is a great tool. But it should be you writing and using your voice, not Claude.

What matters in the end is that audiences don’t care as much about how much content you’re pushing out. They care about the value they get from your content.
